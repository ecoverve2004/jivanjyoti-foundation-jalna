Amazon Reforestation Initiative

Restoring the lungs of our planet.

Project Overview

The Amazon Reforestation Initiative is a flagship project by GreenForest, aimed at combating deforestation and restoring degraded areas within the Amazon rainforest. We work closely with local indigenous communities, leveraging their traditional knowledge and empowering them to lead reforestation efforts. Our approach involves planting diverse native tree species, promoting sustainable agriculture, and protecting vital wildlife habitats. This project not only contributes to carbon sequestration and climate change mitigation but also supports the livelihoods and cultural heritage of the communities involved. Since its inception, we have successfully replanted over 500,000 trees and restored thousands of acres of critical rainforest.

Key Achievements

  • Replanted over 500,000 native trees.
  • Restored 2,500+ acres of degraded rainforest.
  • Established 15 community-led nurseries.
  • Provided sustainable livelihood training to 200+ families.
  • Protected habitats for 5 endangered species.
